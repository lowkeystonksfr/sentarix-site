The real problem

Digital experiences have a hidden issue that everyone feels but no one names clearly. Responsiveness changes from moment to moment. Small variations appear in subtle ways and the experience never feels truly uniform.

Frames that never feel the same

Users move, aim, build, or steer, yet every frame lands a little differently. Some moments feel heavy, others floaty. Frame rate improves but the sense of control never really locks in.

Inputs that do not match intention

The same click or stick movement can feel crisp in one moment and muddy in the next. Users blame their device, the network, or themselves, because there is no clear layer that keeps the feel steady.

Engines that only see raw conditions

Engines react directly to fluctuating conditions. Network variation, platform quirks, and local load all bleed into the user experience, producing a different sense of time from one instant to the next.

What SentariX provides

SentariX sits above the rest of the stack and maintains a stable perceived timeline for your experience. Engines keep running as usual, but the user feels a steady, predictable world.

Perfect step time for perception

SentariX produces a stable step value on every update. Your loop uses that value to advance simulation and presentation so that each frame belongs to the same rhythm, even when real conditions are uneven.

Consistent feel across hardware

Players on very different setups can share the same sense of control. SentariX smooths out the hidden variations that make high frame count users feel different from everyone else.

Drop in engine layer

The engine ships as a sealed SDK with a very small public surface. You feed it samples from your world and read a single step value. No internal details are exposed.

Where SentariX fits first

Any experience that lives or dies on feel benefits from a stable perceived timeline.

Competitive play

Shooting, movement, building, and precision inputs stay aligned with the user’s intention. Consistent feel makes skill more readable and raises trust in the experience.

Training and simulation

When reaction and control matter, uneven feel can break learning. SentariX keeps each scenario aligned with the same internal rhythm, so practice transfers better.

Immersive reality and beyond

Head movement, input, and scene updates can share a unified perceived timeline. The more senses you engage, the more valuable it becomes to keep time itself consistent.

SentariX Engine

A sealed engine that discovers a stable internal rhythm and exposes a single step value for your loop. Everything else stays inside.

High level picture

SentariX watches the conditions your experience lives in and maintains a stable perceived timeline that you can trust. Your code keeps control of gameplay and logic, while the engine ensures that each step belongs to the same internal beat.

Thin public interface

The engine exposes a narrow set of calls. You send in samples from your world and read a step value in microseconds. Internal state and logic stay sealed inside the engine.

Perceived time layer

Rather than reacting directly to raw conditions, your loop advances on a stable step value computed by SentariX. This keeps the feel of the experience uniform, even as the environment moves around.

Engine independent

SentariX does not replace your engine or network stack. It sits above them and feeds a consistent step value into your existing update logic, so adoption can be progressive.

Observable effect

When you hook SentariX into a loop and trace its step value, you see a perfectly flat line even while the raw conditions look noisy and chaotic. That flat line is what the user feels.

Stable step value

Regardless of short term spikes or dips in raw conditions, the step value that reaches your loop remains nearly identical from one update to the next.

Consistent user feel

When frames advance on this stable step value, aiming, movement and interaction take on a very clear, predictable feel. Users notice even before they see any charts.
